BALTIMORE AIR QUALITY ANALYSIS — PRESENTATION SCRIPT
=====================================================

This script walks through each section of the dashboard in order.
No analysis or conclusions — just what each visualization is showing.


1. STUDY AREA MAP
-----------------
This map shows the 16 neighborhoods included in the study, color-coded
into three groups:

  RED dots   — Near both Wheelabrator AND I-95
               (Westport, Cherry Hill, Brooklyn, Curtis Bay,
                South Baltimore, Federal Hill)

  AMBER dots — Along the I-95 corridor ONLY, far from Wheelabrator
               South: Elkridge, Savage, Laurel
               North: Rossville, White Marsh, Aberdeen

  GRAY dots  — Control areas, away from both sources
               (Canton, Fells Point, Dundalk, Hampden)

The dark-red factory icon marks the Wheelabrator Baltimore incinerator
at 1801 Annapolis Rd.

The three groups exist so we can compare:
  - "Near Both" neighborhoods get highway AND incinerator pollution
  - "I-95 Only" neighborhoods get highway pollution but no incinerator
  - "Control" neighborhoods get neither at significant levels
Any difference between the first two groups points to Wheelabrator's
contribution above and beyond normal highway pollution.


2. FACILITY EMISSIONS
---------------------
This section shows what Wheelabrator Baltimore officially reports
emitting, based on EPA National Emissions Inventory filings for 2014
and 2017.

The stat cards at the top are the headline numbers:
  - NOx (nitrogen oxides): 1,101 tons/year
  - SO2 (sulfur dioxide): 308 tons/year
  - PM2.5 (fine particulate matter): 27.3 tons/year
  - CO2 equivalent: 762,683 metric tons/year
  - Total reported emissions: ~3.2 million lbs/year

The two tables break it down further:
  - Major pollutants (measured in tons/year): NOx, SO2, HCl,
    CO, PM, PM2.5, Formaldehyde, VOC
  - Trace pollutants (measured in pounds/year): HF (hydrogen fluoride),
    Lead, Mercury, Nickel, Chromium VI

Each row shows the 2014 value, the 2017 value, and the percentage
change between the two years. Red percentages mean the pollutant
increased; green means it decreased.

The two interactive Plotly bar charts below the tables are the same
data in visual form — hover over any bar for exact values.

The red callout box provides context: Wheelabrator is the single
largest stationary source of air pollution in Baltimore City.


3. WIND ROSES
-------------
Wind roses show how often the wind blows FROM each direction, and
how fast. They are based on 6 years of hourly wind observations
(2019-2024) from BWI airport.

How to read them:
  - Each spoke points in the direction the wind is coming FROM
  - The length of a spoke = how often wind comes from that direction
  - The color bands within each spoke = wind speed classes
  - Longer spoke = more frequent wind from that direction

There are five roses:
  - Overall: all years combined
  - Winter, Spring, Summer, Fall: seasonal breakdowns

These matter because wind direction determines which neighborhoods
are downwind of Wheelabrator on any given day. If the wind is
blowing from the south, neighborhoods to the north receive the
emissions.


4. POLLUTION ROSES
------------------
(Visible after running the full pipeline with AQS data)

These look like wind roses but show something different. Instead of
wind frequency, each spoke shows the AVERAGE measured pollutant
concentration (PM2.5) when wind comes from that direction.

How to read them:
  - Each spoke points in the direction the wind is coming FROM
  - The length of a spoke = mean PM2.5 concentration when wind
    blows from that direction
  - If one spoke is much longer than others, pollution is worse
    when wind comes from that direction

There is one pollution rose per EPA monitoring station. The station's
coordinates are shown in the chart title (latitude, longitude).


5. DIRECTIONAL COMPARISON
-------------------------
(Visible after running the full pipeline with AQS data)

These bar charts directly compare pollutant levels across four
conditions:
  - Downwind of Wheelabrator only
  - Downwind of I-95 only
  - Downwind of both
  - Downwind of neither

For each condition the chart shows the mean, median, and 90th
percentile PM2.5 concentration, plus the number of days in
that category.

One chart per monitoring station.


6. TIME SERIES
--------------
(Visible after running the full pipeline with AQS data)

These are scatter plots of daily PM2.5 readings over the full
date range, with each dot colored by which downwind category
that day fell into:
  - Red = downwind of Wheelabrator only
  - Orange = downwind of I-95 only
  - Purple = downwind of both
  - Gray = downwind of neither

Overlaid on the scatter are 30-day rolling average lines for
each category, making trends easier to see.

One chart per monitoring station. Time series are shown full-width
because the x-axis (years of daily data) needs the space.


7. SEASONAL BREAKDOWN
---------------------
(Visible after running the full pipeline with AQS data)

These are the same directional comparison as section 5, but split
into four panels — Winter, Spring, Summer, Fall.

This matters because wind patterns shift by season (as shown in the
wind roses), so the relationship between wind direction and pollution
levels may also change seasonally.

One chart per monitoring station.


DATA SOURCES
------------
  - Wind data: Iowa Environmental Mesonet, BWI ASOS station
  - Facility emissions: EPA National Emissions Inventory (2014, 2017)
  - Air quality readings: EPA Air Quality System (AQS) monitors
  - Toxics: EPA Toxics Release Inventory via Envirofacts API
